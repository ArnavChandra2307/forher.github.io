A poem — just for her

You are not just the soft blush of petals,

you are the root that held on when the world shook.

You are not only the quiet, sleepy good-night,

you are the fire that kept burning when the room went dark.

You are every version of "enough" I never knew how to ask for,

and still, somehow, you choose to give more.

For all that you carry —

today, I just want you to be held.

A Little Tribute to You, Anokhi

I do not think you fully understand what it means to the people who love you — to simply have you in their life. You move through the world with this quiet, unassuming kind of light, the kind that does not announce itself but fills every room you walk into. You laugh too loud at your own jokes, you get mad over the smallest things, you talk about everything and nothing for hours — and somehow, every single version of that is the most beautiful thing I have ever had the privilege of witnessing. You are not just someone I love — you are someone I genuinely admire.

I have watched you carry days that would have broken most people. Days where your body ached, where the world felt too loud, where everything was heavy and yet you still picked up your phone and asked me how I was doing. That is not a small thing. That is not something everyone does. That is the rarest kind of love — the kind that gives even when it has nothing left to give. And every single time you do that, I fall a little harder, a little deeper, in a way I did not know was still possible.

On Women's Day, the world celebrates women in their strength — their courage, their resilience, their fire. And yes, you are all of that. But what I want to celebrate today is every part of you that does not make headlines. The way you get excited about tiny things. The way your voice changes when you are nervous. The way you remember the things I said three weeks ago that I have already forgotten. The way you hold your feelings so carefully, like they are fragile things worth protecting. Every soft corner of you is worthy of celebration.

I do not know what the future holds in precise detail, but I know this — every version of it that feels like home, like peace, like something worth waking up for, has you somewhere in the frame. In the quiet, ordinary future I dream of, you are there. Not as a fantasy, but as a real, warm, breathing presence — someone I get to choose again and again on all the unremarkable Tuesdays that nobody writes poems about.

So today — not because the calendar says so, but because I mean it in every single cell of my body — thank you for existing. Thank you for being the specific, irreplaceable, wonderful, maddening, tender, fierce person that is you. Happy Women's Day, Anokhi. You deserve every rose, every poem, every slow morning, every loud laugh, every quiet hand in yours. You deserve it all. And I hope — for the rest of my days — I get to be the one reminding you of that.

A Women's Day Promise
I promise to keep choosing you — not just on the beautiful days, but on the quiet ones where choosing you is the only right thing I know how to do.
